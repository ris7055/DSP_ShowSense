The Problem
TV Series Renewal & Cancellation
Streaming platforms invest millions producing TV series, yet renewal and cancellation decisions are made by executives using internal viewership data never shared publicly. Showrunners invest years writing multi-season story arcs only to face abrupt cancellations. Audiences invest emotionally in series without knowing if the story will ever conclude. There is no systematic, data-driven early warning system available to creators, investors, or audiences.
60%
of new Netflix shows cancelled after just one season
$M+
wasted per cancelled series in production costs
How Data Science Solves This
By training a machine learning classification model on publicly available IMDb scores, vote counts, episode runtime, and release year data, we can predict whether a show will be renewed or cancelled — before the official decision is announced.
"Can we build a predictive model using only publicly available data — IMDb ratings, vote counts, review sentiment, genre, episode count — that accurately classifies whether a TV series will be renewed or cancelled before the decision is officially announced?"

Additional Insights & Ethical Considerations
Why this matters
📈
Studios can use early-warning predictions to course-correct struggling shows before the cancellation decision, potentially saving millions in sunk production costs.
🎬
Independent creators can benchmark their pitch against historical patterns — understanding what IMDb engagement thresholds correlate with multi-season renewals.
🌍
Investors and studios in emerging markets like Southeast Asia can use the model to evaluate content acquisition decisions based on predicted longevity.
⚠️
Ethical note: The model's 70.6% accuracy means it is wrong ~30% of the time. It should be used as a supplementary signal, not a definitive decision tool.
Key findings from the data
★
IMDb vote count (audience engagement volume) is the strongest predictor of renewal — more important than the actual rating score itself.
⏱
Episode runtime is the second strongest feature — shows with runtime between 40–60 minutes showed significantly higher renewal rates in the dataset.
📊
Of 1,414 TV shows in the merged dataset, 868 were cancelled (61.4%) and 546 were renewed (38.6%), reflecting the harsh reality of the streaming landscape.
🔍
Breaking Bad (9.5 IMDb, 5 seasons) and Kota Factory (9.3 IMDb, 2 seasons) appear as top renewed shows — validating the model's feature assumptions.
